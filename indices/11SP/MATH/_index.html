MATH 395.00 Topics in Algebraic Number Theory 6 credits

Open: Size: 15, Registered: 8, Waitlist: 0

CMC 319

M T W TH F
12:30pm 1:40pm 12:30pm 1:40pm 1:10pm 2:10pm

Requirements Met:

  • Formal or Statistical Reasoning
Synonym: 26583

Helen M Wong

Study of integers in algebraic extensions of the rationals. Motivated by a failed attempt to prove Fermat's last theorem, we define the ring of integers, examine the failure of unique factorization, and rehabilitate it somewhat by showing that ideals have unique factorization into prime ideals. Further topics may include the finiteness of the class number, units in rings of integers, relations to Galois theory, cyclotomic fields, class number formulas, and the Chebotarev density theorem. Where appropriate for comps projects, links to arithmetic dynamics will be given.

Prerequisite: Mathematics 342 and instructor permission
