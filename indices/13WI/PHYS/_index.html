PHYS 131.00 Introduction to Physics: Newtonian Mechanics 3 credits

Bill Titus

An introduction to classical mechanics using the Newtonian worldview. The kinematics and dynamics of some simple systems including objects in free fall, simple harmonic motion, planetary motion, and the motion of charged particles in electromagnetic fields are investigated using Newton's laws, vector analysis, and the conservation laws of linear momentum, angular momentum, and energy. Comfort with algebra and the integration and differentiation of elementary functions is assumed. Weekly laboratory work.

Prerequisite: Mathematics 111, not open to students who have completed Physics 132, 141, or 142 at Carleton; Concurrent registration in Physics 131L

PHYS 335.00 Quantum Mechanics 6 credits

Open: Size: 32, Registered: 17, Waitlist: 0

Olin 101

M T W TH F
9:50am 11:00am 9:50am 11:00am 9:40am 10:40am
Synonym: 33549

Bill Titus

An examination of the structure of non-relativistic quantum mechanics and how this theory differs from those of classical physics. Topics include the mathematics of Hilbert space, the postulates of quantum mechanics, the motion of a particle in one dimension (including the free particle and the simple harmonic oscillator), the Heisenberg uncertainty principle, and spin. Multidimensional applications will include the harmonic oscillator, the hydrogen atom. Approximation techniques and applications will be presented.

Prerequisite: Physics 228, 229/230 and Mathematics 232. Familiarity with matrix algebra is assumed
